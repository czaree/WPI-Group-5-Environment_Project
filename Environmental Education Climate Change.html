Climate Change

According to National Geographic the definition for climate change is: climate change is a long term shift in global or regional climate patterns. A common misconception of climate change is that climate change and weather are one in the same. However that is not true. Weather changes are measured in a short period of time, like day-to-day or year-to-year. Whereas, climate change is measured on a longer period of time like 2000-2010 compared to 2010-2020. Climate change also differs from weather in the areas that are measured. Climate change measures seasonal temperatures, rainfall percentage of an area, and wind patterns. Weather on the other hand measures short period temperature changes, wind patterns, and barometric pressure.

Because of the common misconception that weather is climate change, most people do not know that weather is a part of climate change. When weather becomes unpredictable it is an effect of climate change in that area. Thus making it difficult for areas who are dependent on certain things like growing crops. If an area that is dependent on crops cannot grow crops because of unexpected temperature levels or rainfall levels, then that area is in danger of becoming one of the many areas where the people are starving. World hunger is an effect of climate change because of the unpredictable changes and the unpredictable intense weathers. For example hurricanes, floods, downpours, and winter storms. If there has ever been an unexpected change in weather patterns the most likely reason for it is climate change.

A commonly thrown around question about climate change is: is climate change a result of human activities? Because of this question many scientist have started studying the Earths atmosphere and the reasons behind why climate change has been as impactful within the past two-hundred years. When studying scientist found that the release of products named fossil fuels and green house gasses are the reason that the progression of climate change has increased. Furthermore, because people have continued to release fossil fuels and greenhouse gasses climate change impacts have increased immensely within the past two-hundred years. Fossil fuels are natural gas, oil, and coal. They are mainly used in factory and c word for making things. Green house gasses, however, have been the major problem of the two. Green house gasses trap the heat from the suns rays within Earths atmosphere. Thus the reason for why global temperatures are rising and why major icy areas are melting.

                                                                     

One major impactful worry is that climate change is actually dangerous to people. Because of the unpredictable nature of weather certain patterns of sickness have come to light. It has been known that the health of the public can be affected by physical, biological, and ecologic systems. But what happens when these systems are thrown off balance?

  • Increased respiratory and cardiovascular disease
  • Injuries and premature deaths related to extreme weather events
  • Changes in the prevalence and geographical distribution of food- and water-borne illness and other infectious diseases
  • threats to mental health

Public Health is continuously going to be impacted by climate change until there is a change within human activities with fossil fuels and green house gasses.
